Learning plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I have modest long term goals at the moment, but with a tentative eye towards Information analytics in the next 10 years. After graduating I'd like to join an established company to build some experience and keep building skills as I work. After establishing myself within that environment, ~2-3years, I would ideally start working towards newer qualifications that would expand my options and opportunity ie analytics.

None of this is set in stone, I'm still seeing which part of development suits me and which I like most. It's almost guaranteed to changed.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

My own learning limitations are mostly set by my willingness to ask for help, and how I manage my focus.

I have strengths in being able to breakdown information into simplistic terms and concepts. This is useful when start out on something tangentily related to a topic I already know a litle about but with things that are new it has to be fed to me in a certain order or from a broad context and then get more specific as we get deeper, so as the course goes on I should get better at grasping concepts as I'll have more references to relate back to. I also have trouble remembering terminology and their applicable contexts

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

How to communicate my thoughts/ideas/understanding of issues/problems/solutions to others effectively. If there's a topic I'm confident in with my knowledge and own practical application I can sometimes stumble around and failing to articulate it in easily digestible terms. This relates back to why I have difficulty asking for help; I've encountered very few people who think like I do.

My commitment to managing my workload in this programme

Time management and schedule

I've set myself up a flexible but determined schedule to work on EDA material each day; Monday and tuesday I go into the Re-leased offices to start the week and get things rolling after the weekend, wednesday and thursday I'll be spending at home where I have my own private space to work out of; it's taking family some adjusting to understand that I'm not available even though I am here but I'm sure they will get there.

I start working on material at 9.30am every weekday, my time is 'blocked' into 1-1.5hour slots including a 15min rest period. I try to do two of these before lunch, then another 2 after lunch. If I'm at Re-leased I'll generally leave around 4.30pm, after dinner I'll practice my typing for about an hour. It's important for me to stick to routine, without one I can easily wander from task to task and not accomplish all that much.

How will you manage to work constructively and productively with other students and the facilitators?

My communication style is very direct, and I've adopted a few ways to take the edge off; ask questions instead of giving direct criticism, include my own personal troubles when someone is frustrated with something, use 'soft' words like maybe/perhaps and choose verbage that is nuetral, and I try to use a more even tone when speaking in person.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

I'm not shy about talking about things that are frustrating or upsetting me. I learned long ago that it doesn't help to keep things inside so I have friends, a partner, and family to talk to about social issues, and my facilitator when it involves other students. Sometimes I just need time to process things aswell.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

Asking for help is something that I've been working on recently on in my own time, I'm a pretty self sufficient person so unless I'm working within a team I see my problems as my own, which is a difficult mindset to break. I'm sure that as time passes and I get to know the facilitators and other students this will becomes less difficult for me.

What I expect from the facilitation team

I expect my facilitator to be my conduit to the course material. Feedback on how I'm doing, general or specific, as well my perception of the material; it goes both ways. I also expect some guidance, and someone to go to with problems whether they're technical or 'core skills' related.
